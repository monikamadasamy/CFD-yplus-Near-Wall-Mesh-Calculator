Applications

External Aerodynamics
Wings, fuselages, cars. Typically y⁺ ≈ 1 with k-ω SST for accurate drag/lift prediction.
🔩
Internal Pipe & Duct Flows
Hydraulic diameter governs Re. Wall functions (y⁺ 30–300) reduce mesh count significantly.
🌡️
Heat Transfer Simulations
Accurate y⁺ ≤ 5 required near heated walls to resolve thermal boundary layer gradients.
🔥
Combustion Chamber Walls
Extreme temperature gradients demand very fine near-wall resolution (y⁺ ≈ 1).

Known Limitations
⚠️ When This Tool's Correlations May Be Inaccurate
🌊 Laminar Flow
Prandtl and Blasius correlations assume fully turbulent flow. Below Re ≈ 5×10⁵ the flow may be laminar and skin friction is overestimated. Use Cf = 1.328 × Re⁻⁰·⁵ (Blasius laminar) instead.
🚀 Compressible Flow (Ma > 0.3)
Incompressible correlations underestimate wall shear stress above Mach 0.3. Density and viscosity vary significantly. Use a compressible solver with temperature-dependent properties.
📐 Pressure Gradient Effects
Flat-plate zero-pressure-gradient assumption breaks down for airfoils, diffusers, and curved surfaces. Adverse pressure gradients cause earlier separation and thicker boundary layers.
🔩 Surface Roughness
Smooth wall assumption is not valid for industrial pipes, engine surfaces, or terrain. Use the Colebrook-White equation for rough-wall skin friction correction.
🔄 Rotating & Curved Flows
Centrifugal and Coriolis effects in turbomachinery alter boundary layer development significantly. Rotating frame corrections and curvature-corrected turbulence models are required.
🌡️ Non-Isothermal Flows
Heat transfer simulations with large temperature differences change fluid properties across the boundary layer. Constant-property assumptions used here introduce error in thermal BL thickness.
CFD Setup Next Steps
🗺 After Generating Mesh Parameters — What to Do Next
  • 1
    Set Inflation Layers in Meshing Software — In ANSYS Meshing, go to Inflation → First Layer Height → enter your Δy₁ value. In OpenFOAM, set firstLayerThickness in snappyHexMeshDict. Use growth rate 1.2 and the suggested layer count.
  • 2
    Run Mesh Quality Check — In ANSYS Fluent, use Mesh → Check to verify skewness and orthogonality. In OpenFOAM, run checkMesh. Fix any cells exceeding the critical limits in the quality table above.
  • 3
    Set Boundary Conditions — Apply no-slip wall condition at all solid surfaces. Set inlet velocity, turbulence intensity (TI from results), and turbulent length scale. Match outlet BC to expected pressure or velocity profile.
  • 4
    Run Simulation and Check Convergence — Monitor residuals. All residuals should drop at least 3–4 orders of magnitude. Check that forces, mass flow rate, or temperature have reached steady-state values.
  • 5
    Check y⁺ Contours Post-Simulation — In ANSYS Fluent, plot y⁺ on all wall surfaces. Verify that actual y⁺ matches your target range. If significantly different, adjust first layer height and re-run.
  • 6
    Perform Mesh Independence Study — Run on at least 3 mesh levels — coarse (2× Δy₁), medium (target Δy₁), and fine (0.5× Δy₁). Compare key quantities. Report results only when changes are below 1–2%.
